Features

Any ERC-20 token can be stored away inside the ebox Liquidity Locker. After selecting a token and setting the desired locking amount and unlocking time, your tokens are safely deposited inside an unbreakable smart contract.

Locks that have been created through this app are always visible to the public. Each lock is assigned a unique ID number and a link, through which it can easily be accessed & shared.

After creating a lock, more tokens of the same kind can be added. The lock ownership may be transferred to another address, and the lock duration can be extended beyond what was set initially.
Once the unlocking time has been reached, tokens can be released from the lock, either entirely or partially.

Frequently Asked Questions

Looking at the process of providing decentralized exchange (DEX) liquidity, the significance of token locking quickly becomes evident:
Whenever liquidity for any token pair is provided on a DEX such as Uniswap or PancakeSwap, the providing person receives so-called "LP tokens". These are regular ERC-20 tokens with a special purpose: LP tokens represent a share of DEX liquidity, meaning that whoever holds them can remove liquidity from the exchange at any time.
This poses a major safety issue. When a project's entire liquidity is withdrawn malevolently, a "rug pull" happens, leaving investors with worthless tokens, essentially stealing people's money.
By locking LP tokens inside a smart contract, rug pulling becomes impossible. Until the unlocking time has been reached, the locked LP tokens cannot be retrieved (and used to remove liquidity).

Tokens are deposited inside an immutable smart contract, which only allows for retrieval by the depositing address once the set unlocking time has been reached.
Functional integrity is guaranteed through open-source code and specialist auditing by CertiK Foundation. Data on every lock created through the ebox Liquidity Locker is publicly accessible through this dashboard.
